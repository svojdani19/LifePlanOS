# 16 — Decision Log

Accepted Technical Decisions (ATDs). Binding until explicitly revisited; new
decisions append here (never edit history — strike through and supersede).

| # | Date | Decision | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| ATD-1 | 2026-07-12 | `ValidationFinding` rows are **persisted source-of-truth records** for integrity results. Derived data: atomically replaced on recompute (generate / physician review / export / on-demand POST); UIs read stored rows rather than recomputing ad-hoc. | One authoritative result set; auditable; cheap reads. |
| ATD-2 | 2026-07-12 | The validation GET endpoint's **summary counts may be computed live** until production profiling shows a performance problem. Stored rows remain authoritative for findings themselves. | Counts are cheap today; premature caching adds staleness risk. |
| ATD-3 | 2026-07-12 | **Object-storage garbage collection on deletion** and **auth rate limiting** are **required before any paid production pilot**. Release gate recorded in [12_DEPLOYMENT.md](12_DEPLOYMENT.md). | PHI retention and credential-stuffing exposure are unacceptable in paid production. |
| ATD-4 | 2026-07-12 | **Frequency/duration plausibility checks** remain roadmap items and must begin as **narrow deterministic rules** (per-category bounds, e.g. office visits ≤ 52/yr, MRI surveillance ≤ 4/yr) — not broad AI inference. | Deterministic rules are testable and defensible at deposition; inference is neither, yet. |
| ATD-5 | 2026-07-12 | **Regeneration supersedes rather than deletes** recommendations with review history. Formal requirement P2.R1 in [15_PRODUCT_ROADMAP.md](15_PRODUCT_ROADMAP.md). | Physician/attorney review actions are legal work product; destroying them is unacceptable. |
| ATD-6 | 2026-07-12 | The **numbered docs/ skeleton (00–17 + epics/) is the canonical documentation structure**; unnumbered duplicates are removed after content migration. | One source of truth; the code implements the docs. |
| ATD-7 | 2026-07-12 | **DOCX remains the canonical report format**; PDF export ships only as a DOCX→PDF conversion when a customer requires it (no separate PDF layout engine). | One typesetting source of truth; the locked Word design is the deliverable attorneys edit. |
| ATD-8 | 2026-07-12 | **Citation quality is enforced at selection time, not just display**, by a single pure module (`citationQuality.ts`): a hard region/procedure/population gate, an explicit multi-factor relevance score with stored reason/claim/limitations, a 10-tier hierarchy with strongest-as-primary, and structured confidence. Relevance metadata rides in the existing `citation`/`socAnalysis` JSON — no schema change. | One defensible, testable source of truth for evidence appropriateness; keyword-only matches and silent article reuse are eliminated. |
| ATD-9 | 2026-07-12 | **The Standard-of-Care module is retired as a user-facing workflow** (nav, tab, report section, export) and replaced by an integrated Medical Necessity & Clinical Evidence engine on each Future Care recommendation. Reusable backend services (guideline retrieval, citations, ranking, traceability) are retained and migrated into the shared engine; `Condition.socAnalysis` remains the guideline store the dossier reads. No schema change. | Eliminates duplicate workflows; makes every recommendation stand alone as physician-quality; keeps the platform out of malpractice/standard-of-care opinion territory. |
| ATD-10 | 2026-07-12 | **Clinical interviews (patient + treating provider) and reviewer credentials are first-class, user-authored inputs** woven into each recommendation's dossier and the report (Current Complaints, Qualifications). The system stores and formats only what a human enters/uploads — it never fabricates interview content. Credential upload is available to any medical-personnel seat (ADMIN/PLANNER/PHYSICIAN_REVIEWER). | Closes the physician-authorship / patient-voice gap vs. a signed physician LCP without compromising the never-fabricate rule. |
| ATD-12 | 2026-07-12 | **A consolidated chart is segmented into typed sub-documents at ingest, stored as `Document.segments` JSON on the parent (not as child Document rows).** One file stays one storage object / one deletion-and-GC unit; segments are display-shaped views (dated section → clinical-with-finding or administrative-with-category) computed by the pure `documents/segment.ts` at ingest and after OCR. Clinical extraction is gated on a clinical signal and rejects patient-facing/boilerplate/OCR noise; administrative pages bearing on the diagnosis or future care (consent, advance directive, DME, work status, financial) are kept and categorized, the rest collapsed to a count — never shown as empty "encounters." | Segments-on-parent delivers the "separate the chart into sub-documents" outcome without the dedup/count/GC blast radius of child rows; gating over a naive sentence grab is the only way to get physician-quality summaries off a noisy 1,000-page OCR scan without fabricating. |
| ATD-13 | 2026-07-12 | **The OCR text cap (`MAX_TEXT`) is 4M chars (~2,000 pages); charts beyond it are re-OCR'd/re-ingested, not truncated.** | A 1,026-page scanned chart was silently cut at ~750 pages, dropping records from the plan; full indexing is required for a defensible review. |
| ATD-11 | 2026-07-12 | **Report authorship derives from a per-case designated "preparing physician," not the case creator.** Only the preparing physician's name, credentials, and CV appear in Qualifications and the signature; credential *upload* stays open to any medical seat (ATD-10), but *report attribution* is singular and explicit. With no designation, the report falls back to the creator's name with generic "under separate cover" language and renders no credentials. | The signer is the physician who takes professional responsibility for the plan; attributing a planner's (or any non-signing seat's) credentials to the report would be misleading at deposition. |
