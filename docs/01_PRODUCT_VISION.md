# 01 — Product Vision

## The problem

A litigation-grade life care plan is one of the most labor-intensive documents
in medicolegal practice. A planner must read thousands of pages of medical
records, build a defensible chronology, tie every diagnosis to objective
evidence, translate diagnoses into decades of future care, price that care from
recognized sources, ground it in literature, obtain physician sign-off, and
typeset a report that survives deposition. Done manually this takes weeks per
case, and quality varies with fatigue: mismatched diagnoses, stale codes,
boilerplate narratives, and unsupported items are exactly what opposing counsel
hunts for.

## The vision

**LifePlanOS is the operating system for life care planning.** One system where
a firm runs the entire case lifecycle — intake → records → chronology →
causation → future care → pricing → physician review → report — with every
artifact traceable to its source page and every recommendation validated for
clinical coherence before it can enter a damages total.

The product's promise is **defensibility per hour**: dramatically less manual
assembly, with output that is *more* consistent and *better* supported than
manual work, never less.

## What makes it different

1. **Deterministic, source-cited engines** — the system quotes records and real
   literature; it never fabricates and never renders a medical opinion.
2. **A built-in integrity check** — diagnosis/region mapping, CPT/pricing
   consistency, literature relevance, and inclusion eligibility are validated
   on every plan; critical defects block final export (DRAFT watermark).
3. **Honest review accounting** — nothing is labeled physician-approved without
   a recorded approval action; the expert stays in control and on the record.
4. **A physician-grade report** — the finished DOCX reads like a premium expert
   report, not software output.

## Who it serves

Life-care-planning practices, physician expert-witness practices, law firms
with in-house planners, and IME/peer-review organizations. See
[03_USER_PERSONAS.md](03_USER_PERSONAS.md).

## North-star outcomes

- A planner produces a defensible draft plan in days, not weeks.
- A physician reviewer disposes of a review queue in one sitting, with evidence
  at hand.
- An attorney can answer "what supports this number?" for any line item in
  under a minute.
- A firm's plans are consistent across planners, cases, and years.

The commercial thesis: firms that experience validated, traceable plans do not
go back to manual assembly — retention follows defensibility. Growth levers and
